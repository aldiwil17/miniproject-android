Given an integer X liter jug and an integer Y liter jug where 0 < X < Y. Both of the jugs are initially empty.

The objective is to get the integer Z liter of water where 0 < Z < Y. The water must be in one of the jug.

There are 3 type of actions that are available to get the water:
  1. FILL a jug with water to full
  2. POUR the water from a jug to another jug
  3. EMPTY all water from a jug
You have to determine the minimum number of POUR action to fulfill the objective.

Example 1

Input

jug1 = 4
jug2 = 7
target = 5

Output

4

Explanation

There are minimum 8 actions to get the objective:
  1. FILL jug 1 with water. Jug 1 is now 4 liter
  2. POUR jug 1 to jug 2. Jug 1 is now empty. Jug 2 is now 4 liter water
  3. FILL jug 1 with water
  4. POUR jug 1 to jug 2. Jug 1 is now 1 liter. Jug 2 is now 7 liter water
  5. EMPTY jug 2
  6. POUR jug 1 to jug 2. Jug 1 is now empty. Jug 2 is now 1 liter water
  7. FILL jug 1 with water
  8. POUR jug 1 to jug 2. Jug 1 is now empty. Jug 2 is now 5 liter water
The POUR action is 4, so the output should be 4.

Example 2

Input

jug1 = 4
jug2 = 7
target = 3

Output

1

Explanation

Below is the minimum actions get get a jug containing 3 liter of water:
  1. FILL jug 2 with water. Jug 2 is now 7 liter
  2. POUR jug 2 to jug 1. Jug 2 is now 3 liter. Jug 1 is 4 liter
Finish in minimum 2 actions. There is only 1 POUR, so the output is 1.
